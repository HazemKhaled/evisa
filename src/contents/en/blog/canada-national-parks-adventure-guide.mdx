Canada boasts some of the world's most spectacular national parks, covering diverse ecosystems from rugged coastlines to towering mountain peaks. With 48 national parks and reserves, Canada offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventure, wildlife viewing, and experiencing pristine wilderness.

## The Big Four: Must-Visit Canadian National Parks

### Banff National Park - Alberta

Canada's first and most famous national park, established in 1885.

#### Iconic Attractions

- **Lake Louise**: Turquoise glacier-fed lake surrounded by peaks
- **Moraine Lake**: Famous for its appearance on the Canadian $20 bill
- **Icefields Parkway**: One of world's most scenic mountain drives
- **Johnston Canyon**: Spectacular waterfall hike through limestone canyon

#### Best Activities

- **Summer**: Hiking, canoeing, rock climbing, wildlife viewing
- **Winter**: Skiing, snowshoeing, ice climbing, dog sledding
- **Photography**: Sunrise at Lake Louise, sunset at Moraine Lake
- **Hot springs**: Banff Upper Hot Springs for relaxation

#### Wildlife Spotting

- **Large mammals**: Elk, mountain goats, bighorn sheep, bears
- **Birds**: Golden eagles, ptarmigan, gray jays
- **Best viewing times**: Early morning and evening hours
- **Safety tips**: Maintain distance, carry bear spray, make noise

### Jasper National Park - Alberta

The largest national park in the Canadian Rockies, known for dark sky preservation.

#### Unique Features

- **Dark Sky Preserve**: World's second-largest dark sky preserve
- **Columbia Icefield**: Largest icefield south of the Arctic Circle
- **Maligne Lake**: Largest natural lake in Canadian Rockies
- **Spirit Island**: Most photographed location in Canadian Rockies

#### Adventure Activities

- **Glacier walking**: Athabasca Glacier tours
- **Stargazing**: Dark Sky Festival in October
- **Whitewater rafting**: Athabasca and Sunwapta Rivers
- **Backcountry camping**: Multi-day wilderness experiences

#### Hiking Highlights

- **Valley of the Five Lakes**: Colorful glacier-fed lakes
- **Mount Edith Cavell**: Angel Glacier viewpoint
- **Skyline Trail**: Multi-day alpine traverse
- **Maligne Canyon**: Deep limestone gorge

### Pacific Rim National Park - British Columbia

Canada's premier coastal wilderness experience on Vancouver Island.

#### Three Distinct Areas

##### Long Beach Unit

- **Endless beaches**: 16 km of pristine sandy coastline
- **Storm watching**: Dramatic winter storms and waves
- **Surfing**: Canada's premier surfing destination
- **Tofino**: Nearby resort town with accommodations and dining

##### Broken Group Islands

- **Sea kayaking**: Remote island camping and paddling
- **Marine wildlife**: Whales, sea otters, seals
- **Indigenous culture**: Traditional territories of Nuu-chah-nulth peoples
- **Camping**: Primitive island camping experiences

##### West Coast Trail

- **Epic hiking**: 75 km backpacking trail
- **Historical significance**: Former lifesaving trail for shipwrecked sailors
- **Challenging terrain**: Ladders, cables, river crossings
- **Reservations required**: Limited permits, book well in advance

### Algonquin Provincial Park - Ontario

While technically a provincial park, Algonquin deserves mention for its iconic Canadian experience.

#### Classic Canadian Activities

- **Canoe camping**: Traditional Canadian wilderness experience
- **Fall colors**: Spectacular autumn foliage viewing
- **Wolf howling**: Guided wolf howl experiences
- **Logging history**: Museums and heritage sites

## Western Canada National Parks

### Yoho National Park - British Columbia

Small but spectacular park in the heart of the Canadian Rockies.

#### Geological Wonders

- **Burgess Shale**: UNESCO World Heritage fossil site
- **Natural Bridge**: Kicking Horse River carved limestone bridge
- **Emerald Lake**: Stunning turquoise mountain lake
- **Takakkaw Falls**: 373-meter waterfall, one of Canada's highest

#### Unique Experiences

- **Fossil tours**: Guided tours to world-famous fossil beds
- **Ice climbing**: Winter ice formations
- **Backcountry skiing**: Pristine powder snow
- **Photography workshops**: Professional landscape photography instruction

### Waterton Lakes National Park - Alberta

International Peace Park shared with Glacier National Park in Montana.

#### Cross-Border Adventure

- **International hiking**: Trails crossing US-Canada border
- **Red Rock Canyon**: Colorful sedimentary rock formations
- **Prince of Wales Hotel**: Historic mountain resort
- **Boat tours**: International cruise on Waterton Lake

#### Prairie Meets Mountains

- **Unique ecosystem**: Where prairies meet mountains
- **Wildflowers**: Spectacular summer wildflower displays
- **Wind energy**: Constant chinook winds
- **Stargazing**: Dark Sky Preserve designation

### Mount Revelstoke National Park - British Columbia

Compact park famous for its meadows and scenic drives.

#### Meadows in the Sky Parkway

- **Summit drive**: 26 km scenic mountain drive
- **Alpine meadows**: Spectacular wildflower displays
- **Hiking trails**: Various difficulty levels from summit
- **Interpretive programs**: Guided walks and presentations

## Eastern Canada National Parks

### Gros Morne National Park - Newfoundland and Labrador

UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing Earth's geological history.

#### Geological Significance

- **Tablelands**: Exposed ancient ocean floor
- **Fjords**: Glacially carved Western Brook Pond
- **Fossils**: Ancient marine life remains
- **Research station**: Active geological research site

#### Adventure Activities

- **Boat tours**: Western Brook Pond fjord cruises
- **Hiking**: Gros Morne Mountain summit trail
- **Cultural experiences**: Newfoundland music and cuisine
- **Photography**: Dramatic coastal and mountain landscapes

### Prince Edward Island National Park

Coastal paradise featuring red sand beaches and gentle landscapes.

#### Coastal Beauty

- **Red sand beaches**: Iron oxide creates distinctive coloring
- **Sand dunes**: Fragile dune ecosystems
- **Lighthouse**: Greenwich Interpretation Centre
- **Anne of Green Gables**: Literary heritage connections

#### Family-Friendly Activities

- **Beach activities**: Swimming, sandcastle building
- **Cycling**: Confederation Trail
- **Bird watching**: Migrating shorebirds
- **Cultural sites**: Historic houses and museums

### Fundy National Park - New Brunswick

Home to the world's highest tides and ancient Acadian forest.

#### Tidal Phenomena

- **Extreme tides**: Up to 12-meter tidal range
- **Tide pools**: Marine life exploration at low tide
- **Flowerpot Rocks**: Hopewell Rocks formations
- **Tidal bore**: Wave traveling upstream

#### Forest Adventures

- **Old growth forest**: 300+ year old trees
- **Waterfall trails**: Over 100 waterfalls in park
- **Dark Sky Preserve**: Excellent stargazing opportunities
- **Camping**: Various campground options

## Northern Canada National Parks

### Nahanni National Park Reserve - Northwest Territories

Remote wilderness protecting the Nahanni River system.

#### River Adventure

- **Canoeing/rafting**: Multi-day river expeditions
- **Virginia Falls**: Twice the height of Niagara Falls
- **Hot springs**: Natural thermal pools
- **Indigenous culture**: Dehcho First Nations traditional territory

#### Access and Planning

- **Fly-in access**: Charter flights required
- **Permits required**: All visitors need permits
- **Guided trips**: Recommended for safety and logistics
- **Season**: Limited to summer months (June-September)

### Auyuittuq National Park - Nunavut

Arctic wilderness on Baffin Island featuring dramatic landscapes.

#### Arctic Adventure

- **Mount Thor**: Highest vertical drop on Earth
- **Akshayuk Pass**: Historic Inuit travel route
- **Glaciers**: Massive ice formations
- **Midnight sun**: 24-hour daylight in summer

#### Extreme Conditions

- **Weather**: Unpredictable Arctic conditions
- **Self-sufficiency**: No services within park
- **Experience required**: Advanced outdoor skills necessary
- **Evacuation insurance**: Mandatory for visitors

## Planning Your National Park Adventure

### Seasonal Considerations

#### Summer (June-August)

- **Peak season**: Crowded but best weather
- **All facilities open**: Full services and programs
- **Wildlife active**: Best animal viewing opportunities
- **Advance bookings**: Essential for popular destinations

#### Fall (September-November)

- **Fewer crowds**: More solitude on trails
- **Fall colors**: Spectacular autumn foliage
- **Cooler weather**: Ideal for hiking
- **Limited services**: Some facilities may close

#### Winter (December-February)

- **Winter activities**: Skiing, snowshoeing, ice climbing
- **Northern lights**: Aurora viewing opportunities
- **Extreme cold**: Proper gear essential
- **Limited access**: Some parks partially closed

#### Spring (March-May)

- **Shoulder season**: Good deals on accommodations
- **Muddy conditions**: Trails may be challenging
- **Wildlife emergence**: Animals coming out of hibernation
- **Weather variability**: Be prepared for all conditions

### Accommodation Options

#### Camping

- **Frontcountry camping**: Drive-in sites with facilities
- **Backcountry camping**: Wilderness camping permits required
- **oTENTik**: Parks Canada's hybrid tent-cabin accommodations
- **RV camping**: Various hookup options available

#### Lodges and Hotels

- **Park lodges**: Historic properties within parks
- **Gateway communities**: Towns near park entrances
- **Luxury options**: High-end wilderness resorts
- **Budget accommodations**: Hostels and motels

#### Unique Stays

- **Yurts**: Circular tent structures in select parks
- **Backcountry huts**: Mountain huts for hikers
- **Indigenous experiences**: Cultural accommodation options
- **Working ranches**: Guest ranch experiences

### Essential Gear and Preparation

#### Clothing and Footwear

- **Layered clothing**: Adaptable to changing conditions
- **Waterproof jacket**: Essential for unpredictable weather
- **Hiking boots**: Proper ankle support for trails
- **Sun protection**: Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen

#### Safety Equipment

- **Bear spray**: Required in bear country
- **First aid kit**: Basic medical supplies
- **Navigation tools**: Maps, compass, GPS
- **Emergency shelter**: Emergency bivy or space blanket

#### Photography Gear

- **Weather protection**: Waterproof camera bags
- **Extra batteries**: Cold weather drains power quickly
- **Tripod**: For landscape and wildlife photography
- **Various lenses**: Wide-angle for landscapes, telephoto for wildlife

### Wildlife Safety

#### Bear Safety

- **Make noise**: Alert bears to your presence
- **Proper food storage**: Use bear caches and lockers
- **Group hiking**: Stay together on trails
- **Know what to do**: Understand bear encounter protocols

#### Other Wildlife

- **Mountain goats**: Don't feed or approach
- **Elk and moose**: Give wide berth, especially during mating season
- **Small animals**: Squirrels and chipmunks can carry disease
- **Marine life**: Observe whales and seals from distance

### Budget Planning

#### Park Fees

- **Discovery Pass**: Annual pass for all Parks Canada sites ($72.25)
- **Daily permits**: $10-20 per day depending on park
- **Camping fees**: $15-40 per night depending on services
- **Activity fees**: Additional costs for boat tours, guided hikes

#### Transportation

- **Vehicle rental**: Essential for most park visits
- **Fuel costs**: Budget for long distances between parks
- **Flights**: Required for remote northern parks
- **Ferry costs**: For island destinations

#### Accommodation and Food

- **Camping**: Most budget-friendly option
- **Hotel costs**: Vary widely by location and season
- **Grocery supplies**: Stock up before entering parks
- **Restaurant meals**: Limited options, premium pricing in parks

### Sustainable Tourism Practices

#### Leave No Trace Principles

- **Plan ahead**: Know regulations and special concerns
- **Travel on durable surfaces**: Stick to established trails
- **Dispose of waste properly**: Pack it in, pack it out
- **Leave what you find**: Don't collect natural or cultural artifacts
- **Minimize campfire impacts**: Use established fire rings only
- **Respect wildlife**: Observe from distance, don't feed animals
- **Be considerate**: Respect other visitors and protect experience

#### Supporting Conservation

- **Park fees**: Directly fund conservation and maintenance
- **Volunteer programs**: Participate in citizen science projects
- **Education programs**: Attend interpretive talks and walks
- **Responsible photography**: Don't disturb wildlife for photos
